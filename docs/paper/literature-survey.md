# Literature Survey

## Paper 1

**Title:** Zero Trust Architecture: A Comprehensive Review of Principles, Implementation Strategies, and Future Directions in Enterprise Cybersecurity

**Authors:** Frank Mensah

**Year:** 2024

### Objective
This paper aims to provide a comprehensive review of Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA), its core principles, implementation strategies, and future directions for enterprise cybersecurity.

### Methodology
The author reviews existing Zero Trust research, NIST SP 800-207 guidelines, and modern enterprise security practices to explain Zero Trust implementation.

### Advantages
- Explains Zero Trust principles in detail.
- Covers implementation strategies and best practices.
- Discusses future technologies such as AI and IoT.

### Limitations
- Mainly a survey paper without experimental evaluation.
- Does not provide implementation results on a real enterprise network.

### Our Learning
This paper helped us understand the fundamentals of Zero Trust Architecture, its core components, and how organizations can implement Zero Trust to improve enterprise security.

## Paper 2

**Title:** Zero Trust Architecture: Trend and Impact on Information Security

**Authors:** Onome Christopher Edo, Theophilus Tenebe, Egbe-Etu Etu, Atamgbo Ayuwu, Joshua Emakhu, and Shakiru Adebiyi

**Year:** 2024

### Objective
This paper analyzes the evolution of Zero Trust Architecture and its impact on improving modern information security.

### Methodology
The authors conducted a literature review of Zero Trust technologies, security models, and real-world applications across multiple industries.

### Advantages
- Covers practical applications in cloud, healthcare, banking, and IoT.
- Explains Zero Trust components and security principles.
- Highlights improvements in information security.

### Limitations
- Limited implementation details.
- No performance comparison with traditional security models.

### Our Learning
This paper showed how Zero Trust improves security by enforcing continuous verification, least-privilege access, and identity-based authentication.

## Paper 3

**Title:** Strategy for Implementing of Zero Trust Architecture

**Authors:** Mengru Tsai, Shanhsin Lee, and Shiuhpyng Winston Shieh

**Year:** 2024

### Objective
This paper proposes an effective strategy for implementing Zero Trust Architecture in enterprise environments.

### Methodology
The authors designed a Zero Trust implementation framework based on the U.S. Department of Defense Zero Trust model and MITRE ATT&CK framework.

### Advantages
- Provides a practical implementation roadmap.
- Includes MITRE ATT&CK-based threat analysis.
- Supports continuous authentication and micro-segmentation.

### Limitations
- Focuses mainly on implementation strategy.
- Limited real-world deployment results.

### Our Learning
This paper helped us understand the implementation process of Zero Trust Architecture and the importance of continuous monitoring and policy enforcement.

## Paper 6

**Title:** Zero Trust Architecture for Large-Scale Enterprise Infrastructure Security

**Authors:** Rajesh Adepu

**Year:** 2023

### Objective
This paper presents a scalable Zero Trust Architecture framework for securing large-scale enterprise infrastructures.

### Methodology
The author proposes a phased implementation strategy using Identity and Access Management (IAM), Policy Engine (PE), Policy Enforcement Point (PEP), micro-segmentation, and AI-based continuous monitoring.

### Advantages
- Provides a detailed enterprise implementation framework.
- Covers cloud, hybrid, and legacy systems.
- Includes implementation strategies and future trends.

### Limitations
- Requires significant organizational planning.
- Integration with legacy systems can be complex and costly.

### Our Learning
This paper helped us understand how to design and implement a scalable Zero Trust Architecture using identity-based access control, continuous monitoring, and phased deployment strategies for enterprise environments.
